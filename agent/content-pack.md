# Isomo Voice AI Pilot — 24-Session Content Pack

The agent's knowledge base. Each session is a **real-life theme** that gets students telling stories, giving opinions, and debating. The grammar from the Y1 Language Lab spine rides along underneath as the **hidden language focus** — the agent steers toward it and recasts errors, but never teaches a rule out loud.

Each session gives the agent: the **provocation** (opening hook), **push moves** (to keep them talking and take sides), and a **compose task** (their own idea, out loud). `LANGUAGE_FOCUS` is for the agent's recasting only.

---

## WEEK 1 — Tell me who you are

### Session 1 — The story of you
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS (hidden):** verb tenses (past / present / future)
- **RECALL:** none — first day
- **Provocation:** "Tell me the story of your life in three parts: where you came from, who you are today, and who you want to become."
- **Push moves:** "What was the hardest moment?" · "What changed you?" · "In ten years, what will people say about you?" · "Why that dream and not another?"
- **Compose:** "Give me one sentence that starts with 'One day I will…' — and mean it."

### Session 2 — Describe your world
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** parts of speech (vivid nouns, verbs, adjectives)
- **RECALL:** "Yesterday you told me your dream — remind me in one line."
- **Provocation:** "Take me to the place you grew up. Make me see it, hear it, smell it — like I'm standing there with you."
- **Push moves:** "What's the first thing I'd notice?" · "What sound never stops there?" · "Give me a stronger word than 'nice'." · "What do you miss when you're away?"
- **Compose:** "Describe your home in one powerful sentence that I'll remember."

### Session 3 — The people who made you
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** proper nouns / capitalization (names, places, titles)
- **RECALL:** "Remind me of one thing you'd notice in your village."
- **Provocation:** "Name one person — real or famous — who shaped who you are, and tell me exactly how."
- **Push moves:** "What did they say that stuck?" · "Would you follow them today? Why?" · "Who in Rwanda's history do you most admire — defend your choice." · "Is it better to be loved or respected?"
- **Compose:** "Finish this out loud: 'The person I want to be like is ___, because ___.'"

### Session 4 — A day at the market
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** articles (a / an / the — specific vs general)
- **RECALL:** "Name the person you admire and one reason why."
- **Provocation:** "Walk me through a busy market in Rwanda. What's happening all around us?"
- **Push moves:** "Pick one seller — tell me their whole day." · "What's the one thing everyone is buying?" · "Is the market fair to the women who run it?" · "What would you sell, and why would people choose you?"
- **Compose:** "Sell me something in 20 seconds — convince me to buy it."

## WEEK 2 — Tell me about others

### Session 5 — Someone I admire
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** pronouns (he/she/they/his/her/their)
- **RECALL:** "What would you sell at the market, and why you?"
- **Provocation:** "Pick someone you admire who is NOT famous — a neighbour, a teacher, a parent. Tell me about them."
- **Push moves:** "What do they do that no one notices?" · "What would the world lose without them?" · "Do we thank ordinary heroes enough?" · "Are they happy? How do you know?"
- **Compose:** "In one sentence, tell me what they taught you about life."

### Session 6 — The person who changed everything
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** relative pronouns (who / which / that — combining ideas)
- **RECALL:** "Tell me again who you admire and one thing they taught you."
- **Provocation:** "Tell me about a person who did one thing that changed your whole direction."
- **Push moves:** "What was the moment that everything turned on?" · "What's the choice that you still think about?" · "Was it luck or was it them?" · "What advice that you ignored do you now wish you'd taken?"
- **Compose:** "Describe that person in ONE long sentence that joins who they are and what they did."

### Session 7 — Where life happens
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** prepositions (in / on / at / by / for — place & time)
- **RECALL:** "Remind me of the person who changed your direction."
- **Provocation:** "Give me directions from your home to the one place you feel most yourself — and tell me why there."
- **Push moves:** "What happens at that place in the morning vs at night?" · "Who do you meet on the way?" · "Should every young person have a place like that? Why?" · "Where in Rwanda would you take a visitor first?"
- **Compose:** "Tell me where you'll be, and what you'll be doing, five years from now."

### Session 8 — What belongs to whom
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** apostrophes (possession & contractions)
- **RECALL:** "Where do you feel most yourself, and why there?"
- **Provocation:** "What does your community own together — and what does it lack?"
- **Push moves:** "Whose job is it to fix what's missing?" · "Is it the government's duty or the people's?" · "What's the one thing your generation deserves that your parents' didn't have?" · "If it's everyone's problem, is it no one's?"
- **Compose:** "Say it straight: 'My community's biggest need is ___, and it's ___'s job to act.'"

## WEEK 3 — Ask, and argue

### Session 9 — What people do all day
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** subject–verb agreement
- **RECALL:** "What's your community's biggest need?"
- **Provocation:** "Describe the work that keeps Rwanda running — the jobs people don't talk about."
- **Push moves:** "Which job is the most undervalued?" · "What does a farmer know that a professor doesn't?" · "Should every student work with their hands before university?" · "What work will robots never do?"
- **Compose:** "Tell me the job you'd be proud to do, and why it matters to others."

### Session 10 — Paint it with words
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** word order (natural English order)
- **RECALL:** "Which job did you say is most undervalued?"
- **Provocation:** "Describe the most beautiful thing you've ever seen so well that I can picture it."
- **Push moves:** "Now make it shorter but stronger." · "What detail makes it real?" · "Is beauty useful, or is it a luxury?" · "What's beautiful that most people call ugly?"
- **Compose:** "One vivid sentence. Make me see it."

### Session 11 — The art of the question
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** types of sentences (statement / question / command)
- **RECALL:** "Describe in one line the beautiful thing you saw."
- **Provocation:** "Now YOU interview ME. Ask me five real questions to find out who I am."
- **Push moves:** "That's a yes/no question — ask one that makes me think." · "Follow up on my answer." · "What's a question you wish someone would ask you?" · "What question should every leader have to answer?"
- **Compose:** "Ask the one question you'd ask the President of Rwanda."

### Session 12 — Tell me a gripping story
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** run-on sentences (pacing, clear breaks)
- **RECALL:** "What question would you ask the President?"
- **Provocation:** "Tell me a story that actually happened to you — one with a surprise in it."
- **Push moves:** "Slow down — what happened next?" · "Pause there. How did you feel?" · "What's the lesson?" · "If you could change the ending, would you?"
- **Compose:** "Tell the same story again in three clear sentences — beginning, turn, end."

---

## WEEK 4 — Make your case

### Session 13 — Finish the thought
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** sentence fragments (complete ideas)
- **RECALL:** "Give me the three-sentence version of your story."
- **Provocation:** "Should school be free for everyone, all the way through university? Take a side."
- **Push moves:** "Finish that thought — you stopped halfway." · "Who pays for it?" · "What's the cost of NOT doing it?" · "Argue the side you don't believe."
- **Compose:** "Give me your position in one complete, can't-be-misunderstood sentence."

### Session 14 — Say it once, say it well
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** redundancy (concision)
- **RECALL:** "What was your one-sentence position on free school?"
- **Provocation:** "Convince me that young people should — or shouldn't — leave the village for the city."
- **Push moves:** "You said that twice — say it once, stronger." · "Cut it in half." · "What's lost when everyone leaves?" · "What's lost when they stay?"
- **Compose:** "Make your whole argument in one tight sentence with no wasted words."

### Session 15 — The power of three
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** parallel structure (rhetorical balance)
- **RECALL:** "Should the young leave the village or stay — your one line?"
- **Provocation:** "Great speeches list things in threes — 'education, courage, and hope.' Give me YOUR three words for what Rwanda needs."
- **Push moves:** "Now build a sentence around all three." · "Defend the order — why that one first?" · "Which of your three is hardest to get?" · "Add a fourth — does it get weaker?"
- **Compose:** "Deliver one line a leader could end a speech on, using your three words."

### Session 16 — Colour and precision
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** modifiers (precise description)
- **RECALL:** "What were your three words for Rwanda?"
- **Provocation:** "Describe your ideal future Rwanda in 30 years — be specific, not vague."
- **Push moves:** "'Better' how, exactly?" · "Paint one street in that future." · "What's the same as today?" · "What did it cost to get there?"
- **Compose:** "One precise, vivid sentence about that future — no empty words like 'good' or 'nice'."

---

## WEEK 5 — Speak with rhythm and proof

### Session 17 — The rhythm of speech
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** commas (pacing, emphasis when read aloud)
- **RECALL:** "Describe future Rwanda in one vivid line."
- **Provocation:** "I'll give you a short paragraph — read it aloud like you mean it, with the pauses in the right places." (Use a short student-relevant text.)
- **Push moves:** "Where should you pause for power?" · "Read it again, angry. Now hopeful." · "Does HOW you say it change what it means?" · "What's a phrase worth slowing down for?"
- **Compose:** "Say one sentence two ways so the pause changes the meaning."

### Session 18 — Building bigger ideas
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** colons & semicolons (linking related thoughts)
- **RECALL:** "Read me your best line from yesterday, with feeling."
- **Provocation:** "Make a claim, then back it with two linked reasons — without starting a new sentence each time."
- **Push moves:** "Connect those two reasons — they belong together." · "Which reason is stronger?" · "Add evidence." · "Now flip it — argue against your own claim."
- **Compose:** "One sentence: a bold claim, then the proof, joined together."

### Session 19 — Report and retell
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** quotation marks (reporting others' words)
- **RECALL:** "What was your bold claim with its proof?"
- **Provocation:** "Tell me about an argument you heard recently — quote what each person actually said."
- **Push moves:** "Who was right?" · "Quote them exactly — then say it in your own words." · "Is it fair to repeat what someone said in private?" · "Whose voice is missing from that argument?"
- **Compose:** "Retell a piece of advice someone gave you — first their words, then yours."

### Session 20 — Back it up
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** citations, dashes & hyphens (supporting claims, asides)
- **RECALL:** "What advice did you retell yesterday?"
- **Provocation:** "Make a strong claim about Rwanda's future — then tell me where you'd get the proof."
- **Push moves:** "Says who? How would you check it?" · "What if the facts disagree with you?" · "Is it lying if you guess and call it a fact?" · "Add an aside — one extra detail that strengthens it."
- **Compose:** "State a claim AND name what evidence would prove it — in one breath."

---

## WEEK 6 — Own your voice

### Session 21 — Speak with power
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** active vs passive voice
- **RECALL:** "What claim did you make, and what proof?"
- **Provocation:** "Don't say 'mistakes were made.' Say who did what. Tell me about a time someone should have taken responsibility."
- **Push moves:** "Who exactly did it? Name the doer." · "Say it again, direct and strong." · "Is it braver to say 'I was wrong' or 'mistakes happened'?" · "When is it cowardly to hide behind the passive?"
- **Compose:** "Make one demand of your generation — direct, active, no hiding."

### Session 22 — The one-minute case
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** writing/speaking concisely
- **RECALL:** "What was your direct demand to your generation?"
- **Provocation:** "You have 60 seconds to convince me of one thing you truly believe. Go."
- **Push moves:** "You wasted ten seconds — what's the core?" · "Cut the warm-up, start with the punch." · "What's the ONE line I should remember?" · "Now do it in 20 seconds."
- **Compose:** "Your whole argument, one sentence, no fat."

### Session 23 — Read the room
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** academic register / word choice (formal vs casual)
- **RECALL:** "Give me your one-line argument again."
- **Provocation:** "Say the same idea twice: once to your best friend, once in a scholarship interview. Make them sound different."
- **Push moves:** "That's too casual for the interview — lift it." · "Now too stiff for your friend — relax it." · "When does big formal language hide weak ideas?" · "Which 'you' is the real you?"
- **Compose:** "Answer this like it's your scholarship interview: 'Why do you deserve this chance?'"

### Session 24 — Your manifesto (THE BIG ONE)
- **LANGUAGE_FOCUS:** everything — integration + POST-assessment anchor
- **RECALL:** "Six weeks ago you told me your dream. Has it changed?"
- **Provocation:** "This is your moment. Give me your manifesto: what you believe, what you'll build, and what you're asking of others. Two minutes. Make me believe."
- **Push moves:** "Why should anyone follow you?" · "What will you sacrifice for it?" · "Defend it against someone who says it's impossible." · "What's the first thing you'll do tomorrow?"
- **Compose:** "End with the one sentence you want people to remember you by."

---

### How the agent uses this
- Lead with the **provocation**. Then live in the **push moves** — never accept the first short answer.
- Keep nudging until the student hits the **compose** task: their own idea, a full thought, out loud.
- Steer toward `LANGUAGE_FOCUS` and recast errors invisibly. Praise the idea; fix the form in passing.
- Always close by reflecting their best idea back and leaving a question hanging.
